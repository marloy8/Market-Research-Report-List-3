<p><p>Type I noninvasive medical sensors refer to devices that directly measure physiological parameters from outside the body, such as heart rate monitors or pulse oximeters. Type II sensors, on the other hand, involve devices that indirectly measure physiological parameters by analyzing signals or data collected from the body, such as blood glucose monitors or ECG monitors. Both types play a crucial role in monitoring and managing various health conditions without the need for invasive procedures, making them valuable tools in modern healthcare.</p></p>
