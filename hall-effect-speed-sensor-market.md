<p><p>A Hall effect speed sensor is a device that uses the Hall effect to measure the speed of a rotating object. The Hall effect is a physical phenomenon that creates a voltage difference across a conductor when a magnetic field is applied perpendicular to the current flow. This voltage difference can be used to determine the speed of a rotating object by measuring the frequency of pulses generated as the object passes by the sensor.</p><p>The Hall effect speed sensor market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.4% during the forecast period. The key drivers for this growth include the increasing demand for accurate and reliable speed measurement in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and industrial automation.
