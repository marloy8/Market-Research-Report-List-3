<p><p>Industrial rock salt is obtained through three main types of processes: Conventional Mining, Solar Evaporation, and Vacuum Pan Evaporation. Conventional Mining involves extracting salt from underground mines using heavy machinery. Solar Evaporation involves collecting salt from shallow ponds where seawater is evaporated by sunlight. Vacuum Pan Evaporation uses a vacuum system to evaporate brine solution, leaving behind solid salt crystals. Each method has its own advantages and drawbacks, leading to different market dynamics within the industrial rock salt industry.</p></p>
