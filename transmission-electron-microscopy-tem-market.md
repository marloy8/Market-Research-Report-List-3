<p><p>Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) is a powerful technique used to study the ultrastructure of materials at a nanoscale level. Conventional TEM utilizes high-energy electrons to create detailed images of samples. Low-Voltage Electron Microscopes operate at lower voltages, reducing sample damage and improving resolution. Cryo-microscopy involves imaging samples at very low temperatures, preserving their native structure. These different types of TEM markets cater to various research needs, offering unique capabilities for studying biological, chemical, and materials science samples with high precision.</p></p>
