<p><p>Automotive climate control systems come in two main types: automatic and manual. Automatic systems regulate the temperature and airflow inside the vehicle without the need for manual adjustment. They often come with advanced features like dual-zone control and humidity sensors. On the other hand, manual systems require the driver or passengers to manually adjust the temperature and fan speed using physical controls. While automatic systems offer convenience and precision, manual systems are typically less complex and easier to operate for some users.</p></p>
