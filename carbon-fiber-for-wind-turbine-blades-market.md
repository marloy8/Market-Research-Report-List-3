<p><p>Regular-Tow Carbon Fiber is characterized by its smaller filaments, typically ranging from 3K to 24K, making it suitable for applications that require intricate designs and high strength-to-weight ratios. On the other hand, Large-Tow Carbon Fiber consists of larger filaments, often exceeding 24K, which are more cost-effective and faster to lay up, making them perfect for mass production of components like wind turbine blades. Each type of carbon fiber caters to different market needs and preferences within the wind energy industry.</p></p>
