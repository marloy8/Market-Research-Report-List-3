<p><p>Vanadium batteries are classified into two main types based on their electrode materials: Carbon Paper Electrode and Graphite Felt Electrode. Carbon paper electrodes have a greater surface area and porosity, allowing for higher energy storage capacity and efficiency. On the other hand, graphite felt electrodes offer better mechanical strength and stability, making them suitable for long-term use. Both types are widely used in various applications such as grid energy storage, backup power supply, and renewable energy integration due to their reliability and performance.</p></p>
