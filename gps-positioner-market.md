<p><p>GPS Positioner market includes two main types: Independent Positioner and Advanced Positioner. Independent Positioner devices rely solely on GPS technology to determine and transmit location data. They are commonly used in basic navigation applications and personal tracking devices. On the other hand, Advanced Positioner devices incorporate additional technologies such as GNSS, IMU, and RTK to enhance accuracy and reliability. They are typically used in professional applications like surveying, precision agriculture, and autonomous vehicles. Both types cater to different market needs and offer varying levels of performance and functionality.</p></p>
